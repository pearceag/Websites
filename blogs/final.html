Week 14: 12/06 - 12/10

Wow it's the last blog post! I can't believe this semester is coming to an end. Even though at times I was very frustrated, in general, I enjoyed this class very much and I am leaving this course knowing more then I did on the first day of class which is a good sign! I really think the design of the course was pretty good. I learned a lot from the labs and struggling them. I would maybe in the beginning of each class have a little lecture (5-10 min) about the key/important aspects from the assigned readings. I know we did a little bit of that towards the second half of the semester, but I think it would've been helpful for me if we little example problems together in class before the labs. What helped me learn the material in the course was doing the labs and the pas. Although, I got frustrated with the labs, towards the end of the semester, they significantly helped me for the final project. What also helped me learn the materials of this course was collaboratively working with other students on the labs and going to Office Hours. I at first was annoyed that our Projects weren't partner projects and Stewart's students had partners for the projects, but I think having no partner for the projects helped me learn the material better. Because I didn't have a partner for the projects, I exactly what's going on in my code and I can design it the way I want to. It's a full reflection of who I am. For me, it was a big learning curve from learning html/css to js/React. I think that maybe we should split these subjects into two courses possibly - having the html/css course be a prerequisite for the js/React course. This way we can go into more depth and learn more complicated functions in html, css, js, and React and build more complicated/useful projects. Also, although I didn't mind the blog posts each week, I know many students that missed several blog posts, so maybe in the future offer up weekly blog posts as an incentive for extra credit.
